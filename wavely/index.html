Wavely —
Designing a smarter coffee chat network

From cold DMs to weekly ripple conversations.

Type App Design
Role Research, Design, Test
Timeline 08/2025 – Present
Location New York, NY

Networking started to feel pretty pointless

In my last years of school, I knew I was "supposed" to network. But every LinkedIn message felt cold and pointless. I opened LinkedIn, tried to reach out, and froze—who do I message, what do I say, and why would they reply? After a few unanswered DMs and hearing the same frustration from students, new grads, and busy professionals, I started Wavely as a way to redesign that experience.

?

How can I improve online networking?

Design goals

High-intent conversations
Make each coffee chat clearly scoped
and mutually valuable instead of vague.
Low-commitment
Fit realistically into busy schedules
and fluctuating social energy.
Ripple-driven
Let one good chat naturally
trigger the next helpful connection.
The Solution

Wavely is a weekly coffee chat network that cuts out cold DMs and endless feeds, matching you with one helpful person at a time and letting each good chat lead to the next.

Weekly Waves

Opt in only when you have time, get one thoughtful match, and show what you're looking for and can offer, so each chat feels like a fair exchange, not a favor.

Warm Starts

Before each chat, Wavely gives you a quick "why you two" summary of what brought you together, so you can open with something real instead of a cold DM.

Refer & Ripples

After a good chat, you can introduce them to someone else. Over time, those warm intros turn into a web of helpful people, not just a longer contact list.

before

Search + Interact with profile

6 out of 8 users said that seeing only one interest on a profile still didn't tell them how to start a conversation. They felt they had "something in common" in theory, but were stuck on what to say first.

after

Ask + Give

So I reframed the profile as an Ask + Give card, clearly showing what each person wants and what they can offer.

Warm Intro

Show 1–2 shared topics and how the person can help you as suggested openers, so it's easier to start a high-intent, mutually valuable conversation.

before

Endless Pending Chats

7 out of 8 users felt overwhelmed by a long list of pending chats and reported that once they had multiple pending conversations, they were unlikely to start any of them.

after

Weekly Waves

Small, time-boxed batches of matches, so networking feels low-commitment, predictable, and easier to fit into their week.

before

End up with a big, shallow network

4 of 8 users still described their networking in V1 as a "big but shallow." Conversations rarely led to the next helpful person.

after

Refer & Ripple Network

After a good conversation, users can refer one more relevant person, and the system uses these referrals to suggest the next match.

Problem

Low motivation for giver/helper to reply

Optional Message & "Buy them a coffee"

Askers can add a short message and send a coffee token, giving givers a clear reason and a small reward for helping.

Problem

Avoid zombie users during matching

Expiring Matches

If someone doesn't reply within 12 hours, the match expires and their weekly chance reduces.

Avoid ghosting / unrepling during chat

10 mins chat

encourage giver to help the person who receive a coffee will gain a coffee, and they can give coffee to someone else

Swipe to join the wave
